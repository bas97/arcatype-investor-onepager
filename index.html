The problem
01
For brands: discovery is broken
Attention is fragmented. CAC is rising. Wholesale is less predictable. The brands that make things meant to last need time and trust — but the market rewards constant output and pay-to-play reach.
02
For brands: no intelligence layer
WGSN costs €25K/year and targets enterprise. There is no mid-market product giving independent brands competitive benchmarks, category trend signals, or digital product data readiness.
03
For consumers: discovery is noise
Finding brands worth caring about means endless scrolling and algorithm-driven feeds. There is no place built for the consumer who values quality, craftsmanship, and intention over volume.
The result: the market rewards loudness, not quality. Brands making exceptional products are at a structural disadvantage against mass brands — and no curated space exists for the people who would buy differently if they could find differently.
The solution
Human-curated database
Every brand passes a taste filter
Brands are scored across Craft, Character, and Care — anchored to real data sources, not analyst opinion. The result is a standard the industry can reference.
Intelligence outputs
Gap analysis, benchmarking, trend signals
Brands receive competitive benchmarks, market white space reports, and cross-brand tastemaker affinity data — insights that only exist inside Arcatype.
Offline moments
Events as acquisition + enrichment engine
Curated brand experiences convert attention into trust and generate the community signals that make the database richer with every edition.
Online and offline reinforce each other. Offline builds community and trust with a highly focused audience. Online delivers the intelligence brands pay for. Both are needed to make the system work.
